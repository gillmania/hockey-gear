Here you'll find all the sizing guides for CCM player gear. We've gathered all the measurements and details to help you find the perfect fit for your equipment.

Sizing Guidelines: The chest measurement should be taken from just below the arm pits. Match the player's chest size to the shoulder pad that corresponds. Also take into consideration the level of play. Shoulder pads should fit snugly while the vital tips of the shoulder must be properly under the shoulder caps. The bicep pads should not interfere with the player's elbow pads. If you want more in-depth advice on selecting and sizing shoulder pads, please contact our customer support for personalized guidance.

Sizing Guidelines: Shin guards are measured best while the player is sitting. Measure from the center of the kneecap to the top of the skate boot. Match the player's shin size to the inches of the shin guard. Shin guards, which are either too long or too short, will result in the knee or instep being exposed and unprotected. Shin guards should be secured with shin guard straps or tape, never tight hockey socks. If you want more in-depth advice on selecting and sizing shin guards, please contact our customer support for personalized guidance.

Sizing Guidelines: Measure from the back of the elbow to wrist crease with arm at 90°. Match the player’s measured size to the size of the elbow pads by inches. Determine the level of play and the level of protection necessary. When fastened securely, there should be no gap between the pad and either the biceps extension of the shoulder pad or the cuff of the glove. The elbow pad should not slip or move while fastened. Players who wear a short cuff style glove should choose the longer model of elbow pad.If you want more advice, contact our customer support for personalized guidance.

Sizing Guidelines: Finding the right fit for your CCM hockey pants is key to comfort, mobility, and protection. Start by measuring your waist and hips, then use the official CCM size chart to find your ideal size.Consider your playing style—tighter fits offer more mobility, while looser fits provide extra coverage. Your pants should sit comfortably on your waist, meet the top of your shin guards, and allow free movement.Whenever possible, try them on before purchasing to ensure a secure, comfortable fit. Following these steps will help you choose the best CCM hockey pants for your game.

Sizing Guidelines: Getting the right fit for your CCM hockey helmet is essential for both comfort and protection. Start by measuring the circumference of your head with a flexible tape measure, positioning it about an inch above your eyebrows and ears. Next, refer to the CCM helmet size chart to match your measurement with the appropriate helmet size. Once you've selected a size, try the helmet on to ensure a snug fit without discomfort or pressure points, making sure it sits level and covers your forehead adequately. Finally, adjust the chinstrap and any additional fastenings according to the manufacturer’s instructions to secure the helmet with minimal movement.
